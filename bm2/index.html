Blue Marble 2012
Published 6 March 2019

If you've ever been so unfortunate as to fall down the flat earth rabbit hole online, you may have stumbled across this image:
All images are official Nasa pictures of Earth, as released through the mainstream media outlets and major news agencies of the world. Questions: How big is America? What colour are the oceans, and the land? Why is there never any real video of the Earth spinning, only stills? Why are the images so varied? Do you really believe this is where you live? Do you actually trust Nasa?

This argument isn't exactly convincing—it should be well known that photographs taken by different cameras with different settings will show drastic variation in the color and lighting of their subject—but while this does explain most of the variation among these images of Earth, one of them sticks out like a sore thumb: The Blue Marble 2012.
This view of North America makes it look drastically larger than it would seem that it should be compared to any globe I've ever seen, almost as if it's mocking the people who patriotically overestimate the size of the United States.
